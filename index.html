Free Online Photo Scanner & Splitter

Welcome to PhotoSplit Studio, your free browser-based solution to extract, crop, and split multiple printed photos from a single scan. If you have been digitizing old family albums by placing 3 or 4 photos on the scanner glass at once, you know how tedious it is to manually crop them later. Our tool automates that completely — in seconds, directly in your browser, with zero uploads and complete privacy.

How to Split Scanned Photos Automatically

Getting started takes less than a minute. Here is the complete step-by-step process:

  1. Scan multiple photos at once: Place your printed photos on your flatbed scanner, leaving a small gap between each one so their edges do not touch. Scan at 300 DPI or higher for best results.
  2. Upload the scan to PhotoSplit Studio: Drag and drop your scanned image file (JPEG, PNG, TIFF, BMP, or WebP) into the upload zone. You can also click the zone to browse for files.
  3. Run Auto-Detection: Click the Detect Photos button. Our client-side computer vision algorithm scans every pixel of your image to find the boundary between the scanner background and each individual photo. Coloured bounding boxes appear around each detected region instantly.
  4. Fine-tune if needed: Drag the corner handles to adjust any bounding box. Use Add Crop to manually draw a box for any photo that was not auto-detected. Use arrow keys to nudge a selected box one pixel at a time.
  5. Save your individual photos: Click Save All to download every detected photo as a separate file, or click any thumbnail in the strip at the bottom to save just that one.

Supported Image Formats

PhotoSplit Studio supports all major image formats produced by flatbed scanners, all-in-one printers, and camera scanning apps:

  • JPEG / JPG — the most common format for scanned photos. Offers small file sizes with adjustable compression quality.
  • PNG — lossless format, ideal when you need to preserve fine detail without compression artifacts.
  • TIFF / TIF — the professional archival format used by high-resolution flatbed scanners. Even large multi-page TIFFs are fully supported.
  • BMP — uncompressed Windows bitmap format, often produced by older scanner drivers.
  • WebP — the modern format used by Google Photos and some mobile scanning apps.
  • GIF — legacy format support included for older collections.

Your split photos can be exported as JPEG (quality 10–100%) or lossless PNG — whichever suits your archiving workflow best.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PhotoSplit Studio really free?

Yes, completely free — no account required. There are no subscriptions, no premium tiers, and no limits on the number of photos or scans you can process. The service is supported by non-intrusive Google advertisements.

Do my photos get uploaded to a server?

No. PhotoSplit Studio is a fully client-side web application. When you open a file, it is loaded into your browser local memory via the FileReader API. All processing happens on your own device. Nothing is transmitted over the network. You can use this tool offline after the page has initially loaded.

What is the maximum image size supported?

There is no hard file size limit enforced by PhotoSplit Studio. The practical limit is your device available RAM. Most modern computers handle scans up to 100 MB without issues. For very large TIFF files, Chrome or Edge generally offer the best memory performance.

How many photos can be detected in a single scan?

The detection engine can identify as many individual photo regions as are present in the scan image, with no artificial cap. In practice, most flatbed scanners accommodate 4–6 standard 4×6 prints per scan. We have tested successfully with up to 12 small wallet-size prints in a single high-resolution TIFF file.

Can it straighten photos rotated at steep angles?

Yes. The auto-straighten algorithm detects the dominant edge angle of each bounding region and de-skews it. It reliably handles rotations of approximately ±30 degrees. For photos placed nearly upside-down, a final manual rotation in a viewer after downloading may be needed.

Does it work on iPhone, iPad, and Android?

Yes. PhotoSplit Studio works on modern iOS and Android browsers including Safari, Chrome, and Firefox Mobile. You can upload photos directly from your camera roll or files app. Processing speed depends on your device CPU, but most scans complete within a few seconds on any modern smartphone.

How is PhotoSplit Studio different from AutoSplitter?

AutoSplitter is a well-known Windows desktop application. PhotoSplit Studio runs entirely in your browser — no installation needed, works on any OS (Windows, Mac, Linux, ChromeOS, iOS, Android), completely free, and with the same local-only privacy guarantee as a desktop app. It is the ideal cross-platform alternative to AutoSplitter.

Can I convert scanned film negatives to positive images?

Yes. PhotoSplit Studio includes a Convert Negative toggle in the Enhancements panel. After loading your scanned film negative, enable the toggle before saving — the tool inverts the colour channels to produce a natural positive photograph. This works on C-41 colour negative film as well as black-and-white negatives. For best results, scan negatives against a bright white or neutral grey background.

Can I use it for commercial purposes?

Yes. PhotoSplit Studio is free for both personal and commercial use. If you are a professional digitization service or photography studio needing a high-volume or custom solution, please contact us.
